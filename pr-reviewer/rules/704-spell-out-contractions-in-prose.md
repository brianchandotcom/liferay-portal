# 704: Spell Out Contractions in Prose

In prose — comments, documentation, log and exception messages, labels, and commit messages — spell out contractions. Write "do not" for "don't", "cannot" for "can't", "it is" for "it's", "does not" for "doesn't", and "will not" for "won't". Possessive forms such as "Liferay's" or "the user's" are not contractions and stay as they are.

**Rationale:** The spelled out forms read as deliberate and uniform, and they avoid the apostrophe confusion (its versus it's, whose versus who's) that contractions invite.

A violation is a contraction in prose where the full form should be written.
