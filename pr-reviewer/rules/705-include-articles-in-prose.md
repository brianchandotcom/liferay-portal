# 705: Include Articles in Prose

In a full prose sentence, include the definite and indefinite articles a noun requires. Write "raise an exception", not "raise exception"; "using the source formatter", not "using source formatter". Articles are commonly dropped in headlines, code identifiers, and short bullet labels, but a full sentence keeps them.

**Rationale:** A missing article reads as terse, translated, or unfinished. The article lets the sentence read the way a person would actually say it.

A violation is a full prose sentence that drops an article a noun requires.
